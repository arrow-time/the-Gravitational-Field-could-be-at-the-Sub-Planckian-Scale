# the Gravitational Field could be at the Sub-Planckian Scale

Why the Gravitational Field Resides at the Sub-Planckian Scale
To date, no experimental or observational evidence has been found for the quantization of the gravitational field at the Planck scale — neither gravitons have been detected, nor has any discrete excitation spectrum of gravity been observed at Planckian energies. This fact suggests that the fundamental degrees of freedom of gravity may not reside at the Planck scale itself, but rather deeper within a sub-Planckian structure.

At the same time, electromagnetic waves exhibit well-defined spin degrees of freedom at the Planck scale, and their propagation must remain compatible with the geometric structure of spacetime. In the two-layer base space proposed in this paper, this compatibility is achieved through 2 levels: Planck-scale Space Elementary Quanta (SEQ) carry spin and gauge degrees of freedom, satisfying the requirements of electromagnetic theory, while the gravitational field is attributed to the collective response of sub-Planckian elastic constituents that connect the SEQ. The spin degrees of freedom of SEQ and the deformation degrees of freedom of sub-Planckian elastic bonds are decoupled in low-energy states, allowing the spin propagation of photons to remain undisturbed by the elastic response of gravity.

The Planck-scale quantum layer is posited as a topologically stable excitation of the sub-Planckian metric field, thereby achieving compatibility of degrees of freedom.

Consequently, the dynamical carrier of the gravitational field is the sub-Planckian substrate, rather than Planck-scale quantum excitations. This explains why quantization of gravity at the Planck scale has remained undiscovered — gravity simply does not operate at that level.
